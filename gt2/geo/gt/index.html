Formerly Great Links Which Are Now Dead (top)

The Web does not believe in history, but I do. So I will persist in keep this annotated list of sites and pages which used to be very interesting, but have vanished for various reasons.

  • A tuning guide and more, by the (infamous?) Jet Jaguar.
    A guide prepared by an engineer who knew some of the theory behind what he wrote. Probably he changed ISP's and didn't move his pages.
  • Gabe's Gran Turismo pages. Contains some of the earliest attempts to document GT2.
    Another not-so-young GT fan. I suddenly lost contact with him soon after the release of GT2. I suspect he had re-evaluated his priorities and decided to entirely give up GT.
  • granturismo.com - sometimes info, usually a message-board
    granturismo.com domain itself was sold, and message-board relocated to granturismoforum.com
  • A concise page, not on the ring.
    jlirish's Gran Turismo information, with a personal touch quite likely inspired my own diary pages
  • An Okay page, difficult to spot on ring. Probably going away soon, though.
    Strangely, the pointer to his relocated site remains on a university web server, while the site itself has vanished
  • Gran Turismo Central
    apparently a victim of the great XOOM cleanup/reorganization
  • Gran Turismo 2 Central
    apparently a victim of the great XOOM cleanup/reorganization
  • More Recent Gran Turismo FAQ (text)
    what did I expect, linking to a non-privileged port on an MIT server?
  • A brief review of Gran Turismo and interview with its designer.
    This was wonderful, with a great picture of Kazunori Yamauchi and his Mitsubishi Lancer
  • Official PlayStation Gran Turismo site
    Even the official playstation.com site doesn't maintain its own history?!?!?!
    Hmm. Well, sorry, I guess they do have new links here. (They changed them a couple of times, actually).
  • Early Gran Turismo FAQ (text)
    Oh well. I don't think I even bothered to save a copy of that one.
  • Luke Skywalker's GT2 Game Shark pages.
    It's so sad to see this classic repository of GameShark expertise apparently disappear.
  • The Followers of GTRC
    This message-board never really got going anyway, as far as I could tell.

These pages were initially created since I thought Gran Turismo could be a focus to help me explore some ideas about using HTML. Quite clearly, the rate at which I am learning anything by creating these pages has diminished a lot. Or, in other words, they have probably got out-of-hand.

Consequently these pages may soon disappear with little or no notice. (Other than this). Updates have become very sporadic recently. That is not necessarily a totally bad thing.

I actually went to the effort of finding semi-professional hosting. Until I have everything worked out, you will find some links which do not work properly, such some links in the diary. This is partly because the directory structure needed changing for the new site, and partly because CGI pages cannot be hosted there.

I actually did manage to get the Search facility for the diary to work. (It actually searches the original "mirror" copy, but returns links to the pages here).

To learn about HTML, the content, of which I have too much already, is inimportant. Consequently, you might notice these pages in future changing more in details of presentation, and less in content.
