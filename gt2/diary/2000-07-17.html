(GT2) Evolution of a GameShark Code, Conclusion (For Now)

As I demonstrated in previous entries, I created a GameShark Code for GT2 which causes you to win as prize cars hybrids constructed from your garage. These codes can only possibly work for GameShark Version 3.00 or higher, because they depend upon a special instruction implemented only those versions of game enhancer cartridges.

This last version is just a tweak of the previous one. It uses nine garage entries to contribute to the hybrid, rather than just six.

The first version is for the original 1.0 North American version of GT2:

So this is actually the code I wanted!!!

With the code active, arrange that nine cars you want to combine, as indicated, are at the top of your garage. Often you want to have duplicate copies of cars if there are only certain things you want to combine. In an extreme case, you might put eight copies of a BMW in the first eight slots, and put a car with nice after-market rims in the ninth slot, and then your prize will be the BMW fitted with the custom rims.

There are still some common hybrid operations you cannot do with this, such as setting your own gear ratios, or changing the drivetrain. But this is a good way to try out basic parts and ideas, try to debug problems without having to manipulate the shark and hex codes in its memory editor.

A caveat applies to all these codes. I think they strain the limits of the GameShark, and about once every 20 or so attempts, I get a dreaded No Name No Name car instead of the desired hybrid. Often this happens twice in a row, and then tends to clear up. I think it is a timing problem, aggravated perhaps by the length of my code, or, perhaps, I think I heard once, by the fact I have so many codes now for Gran Turismo 2, even though most of them are not active.
