(GT1 Version 1.1) GameShark Addresses and Codes for Simulation Competitor Lists

In Simulation Race mode, you can track down the competitors in the event structures. Here is the Normal Series. 

   80090A48 -> 8008C228 -> "hnsnn" (Acura NSX TypeSzero)
   80090A4C -> 8008C4D0 -> "t-rdn" (Toyota TRD3000GT)
   80090A50 -> 8008C1F0 -> "siphn" (Subaru Impreza)
   80090A54 -> 8008C1D0 -> "afa7n" (Mazda FD RX-7 Aspec)
   80090A58 -> 8008C110 -> "nr34n" (Nissan R34 Skyline GT-R)
   80090A5C -> 8008C178 -> "mlnnn" (Mitsubishi Lancer EvoIII GSR)

Now you can't use the GS Pro memory editor to patch these because by then it is too late.

But you can create a boot code to change some of those values.

When you do that, it is easiest to change the pointers.

For instance, I created a code. 

   D0090A48 C228
   80090A48 C1D0
   D0090A4C C4D0
   80090A4C C1F0

This replaced the NSX with the FD RX-7, and the TRD 3000GT with the Subaru Impreza. However, I'm such a rotten driver, I still lost again the resulting field (which included two RX-7's) when I entered a Lancer Evolution IV GSR. I won the first three races easily, but got completely destroyed on the third, coming sixth, and then finished third in the last race. I first-lap accident put me behind, and I couldn't catch the two RX-7's, but I think I was falling behind them, and they finished further ahead of me than the accident itself would account for.

Note that you cannot set entrant cars to be things which the game does not expect for that event. If, for instance, you arrange that the NSX is replaced by a Viper GTS, you get a strange, 174 bhp FF Viper GTS weighing about 1700 pounds. A refugee from the FF or Lightweight Series, I think, as the game gets mixed up. The wheels are the wrong size; the car looks sort of like a Honda CRX or something running around with a Viper body shell mounted on top of it!
