Observations while gathering License Test Criteria for a web page.

Gotta love that IA-8 gold criterion, specified to .001 second. 1:55.43 is not good enough!

<<-- License Test Progress-->>

While gathering that data I needed to try license tests, to get access to the records.

I passed B-8 and IA-1 on the first try, but failed A-8.

I inferred the IA Bronze times by rounding up my recorded times. I didn't actually try each one.

Later in the evening while trying to work out the used car stocking algorithm, I kept failing A tests. But I eventually passed each one in fairly short order, A-4,A-5,A-3,A-6,A-7 in that order. I actually got a silver on A-7, but didn't save it. I think I can do it again. I passed my dreaded A-3 by a reasonable margin with manual transmission this time. I seem to subconsciously get more speed out of the initial series of turns. One problem with Gran Turismo, though, is it seems to make me tense and nervous when I am driving my real car at highway speeds. I got down to the right second in A-8, but never persisted to passing, since I had stayed up too late already.

By the time you're above 1400 days, used car stock only changes every 10 days, on the 10 days, and all three manufacturers I checked (Toyota, Mazda and Honda) changed on the same day. I think it changes more frequently early in the game. I got that impression when I did a similar experiment from a fresh game, but I didn't take any notes. Gee, I think I wish I had a laptop if I'm going to make those sort of notes in detail. Which I am very tempted to start doing.

I could be chintzy and put in some second-place best laps too, but I think your rule should be that all times shown as 1st, 2nd, 3rd were at one time 1st, if you see what I mean. That is, you should only add 1st place times, rolling down previous 1st times to 2nd and 3rd. Which quite likely means you'll invalidate my times based on submitted but unprocessed data.

Of course I was using highly unsuitable machinery for the races. I gathered the times last night while testing the prize Supra RZ. My wife says it is not really gold, but gold is a good enough description until someone comes up with a better concise colour description. It has orange or copper tones to the colour, and my wife suggested it is sort of an "iridescent pumpkin" colour.

Have you noticed how in some races, especially on the High-Speed Ring, if you leave the action going for a long time after the end of the race, eventually almost every car will come up behind you and bump your bumper before passing, as if it's some sort of congratulatory ritual?

<<-- Tuning Advice-->>
||--Brake Balance Controller-->>

I should do some radical comparison tests with brake balance controller.

I was reading

  

http://www.cheatcc.com/psx/sg/gran_turismo_ia_class.txt

and it seems to imply that the "brake balance controller" also affects the total power of the brakes. I.e you are not only affecting the ratio, you also affect the power. If 100% brakes is too much at a particular setting, I guess this is not a problem, since whatever absolute amount you choose above that, you will need to compensate by applying less force. But if you can actually use more than 100% braking, you should raise both settings so as to maintain close to the same ratio, I guess. Default is 7/7 which may be significant.

Of course, the FAQ author could have been mistaken in his interpretation, hence the need for some testing.

The section

  

http://www.ent.ohiou.edu/~albrnick/gt/simulation/modifications/tuning.html#Brakes

doesn't commit itself one way or another on this issue, but, then again, I see it is actually from mailto:chmilnir@worldnet.att.net
