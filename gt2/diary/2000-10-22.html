After the start, I soon settled into fourth place, ahead of the other EXIV 200GT, but worked my way past the Lancer into 3rd before the end of the first lap. On lap 4 the R32 dropped back from the lead a let the NSX pass it, and in the process obstructed me a bit.

Because of the AI pit stops, I got into first place by my first pitstop, but dropped to 3rd after it.

Similarly, I had gained first place by my second pitstop, but dropped to 2nd after the stop. My time was about 50:55 as I exited the pit, on schedule for about 10 seconds under 1-42, and that with the extra pitstop included.

Never-the-less, I dropped from first to 3rd on my third pit stop too. Each time I had gained the lead, I just had the NSX in sight, and then it pitted. The NSX pitted once again on lap 53, and I got the lead and managed to hold on to win.

I won Cr300,000, and a black/green Castrol Supra GT.

This was a very close race.

                (max, much less)

The use of much less than maximum rear downforce really seems to allow low-powered FF cars to accelarate and go much faster. It does not seem to adversely affect cornering; in fact, it seems it can even help since the physics model pulls the car around the corner by its front wheels, and the low rear downforce seems to let the tail come around faster after it. Reducing the front camber seems to help acceleration, too.
