Prize and Special Model Car List

This list contains prize cars won from races and the special models that can be bought from most manufacturers.
The list shows important features of the cars as well as links to the race series where they can be won. There is also a link on the cars name to more information about the car.

Some cars are listed twice. Cars that can be race modified are listed in both standard and race modified (and full tune) trims. Special model cars that can be tuned for more power are also listed twice. This includes the GT40, the only car that comes as a race model but can tuned for more power.

Notes

Race Mod

This indicates if the car is a race model or normal car. There are several codes used to signify the race modified status of a car:

  • R is a car that comes in race form (special model or prize car).
    This will have adjustable downforce and maximum weight reduction.

  • r indicates a race-like car.
    ie a non-race car which has high downforce and can be considered as a race model.
    Like a normal car, the downforce is fixed, but it is much higher than normal (above 30 front and rear).
    In some cases GT2 treats these as race cars, such as entering them in races which has all race cars. But in other ways they are treated as normal, like being able to enter Normal events in a manufacturer series.
    Some of these can still be race modified and most can have weight reduction.

  • X is a normal car that can not be modified with a race body
    It can have further weight reduction.

  • Y is a normal car that can be modified with a race body

  • M is a car that has been modfied to have a race body
    The original car may have been either normal (M) or a race like (r).

In general, race cars (both R and M) are the best, the lighter weight and better downforce make them handle, accelerate and brake better. The race-like (r) are also very good. The fixed downforce is usually not a problem except at the Test Course.
There is no practical difference between normal cars that can (Y) or cant (X) be modified, until the car is modified. The modifiable cars are good cars to build up, since they can be finished in race trim and compete against full race cars. This is especially handy for some of the less powerful cars, as it can get you race cars under some of the lower power limits.

More r race-like cars can be found in the Interesting Car list.

Price

The price given for cars is the sell price in the garage. Special models will cost 4 times this amount to buy.

Turbo

This indicates if the car is Turbo or NA (normally aspirated).
Some cars can be both, these are NA cars that are allowed a Turbo tune. While many NA cars allow just a turbo tune, some are also capable of both a NA and Turbo tune.
Note: GT2 considers Supercharging as NA. Cars such as Jaguar XKR and the Mercedes SLK Kompressor have superchargers, but both as treated as NA in the game.

Tuned

This shows the type of tuning that is applied to the car. This is for cars that are listed twice, with For some cars both Turbo and NA tuning are available, but generally the Turbo is used since it produces more power.
