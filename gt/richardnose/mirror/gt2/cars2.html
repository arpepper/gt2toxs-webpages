Notes

Power, Revs and Torque

This shows the maximum power, the revs at which that occurs, the maximum torque and its revs, then the maximum revs of the engine.
The maximum revs is not the same as the redline, it is the most revs the engine will do. When using the automatic gears the engine will normally only reach this if it revs out in top gear.
Also the maximum revs of some cars is increased with an Engine Balance tune. This does not change the redline, or the shift point of the automatic gearbox. But for a few cars where maximum power is at the redline, it can help, especially if used with manual gears.

Tuning

This shows the type of tuning that can be applied to the car. It shows both the type (N or T) and the maximum level of tune possible for that type.
For some cars both Turbo and NA tuning are available, but generally the Turbo is used since it produces more power.
'-' indicates that the car can be tuned for more power, but that a Turbo or NA tune is not available (it may use Muffler or Computer Chip)
If this field is blank then the car can not be tuned for more power (race cars can not be tuned - except the GT40!)

Tuned Power

This gives the maximum power available with tuning - excluding permanent tunes (Port Polish, Engine Balance, Increase Displacement).
This indicates the maximum power that can be achieved, while keeping the car capable of being returned to its original power.

Note that for race modified cars (M), the power listed already includes both the listed tuning and any possible permanent tunes.

Downforce

These two fields show the maximum front and rear downforce.
For race cars, these are adjustable, but only the maximums are shown, as generally more downforce is better.
The r race-like cars are noticable for their high downforce. But unlike proper race cars, this is fixed.
Normal cars have much lower downforce. While there is some variation in these, generally it doesnt make a huge difference between a downforce of say 21 or 25.

Ride Height

This gives the range of possible ride heights when the car is fitted with an adjustable suspension.
Generally the lower the minimum ride height the better the car handles. While having ride height set to the minimum is usually not the ideal setting, having a good range of low settings gives better tuning options.

Rally cars are usually obvious by their much higher ride settings.
In fact the Legacy Wagon and Impreza Wagon both have minimums higher than the maximum of several cars!

Note: the Nissan R390 GT1 Road Car can not get adjustable suspension. So ride height (as well as all other suspension settings) are fixed!
This may be a bug, as normally these parts (as well as tyres) are already fitted to special models. The Nismo LM Road Car also comes without any added parts, but at least for it the suspension upgrade is available.

Gears

This is the number of gears available as standard on the car. More gears is better!
Most cars come with either 5 or 6 gears, but a few come with less. Fitting a fully custimized gearbox will give the car at least 5 gears, but it wont increase it to 6.

Buying an adjustable gearbox is a very good idea, as is learning how to setup the gears!
If the car has less than 5 gears, then it is doubly important as it will give the car much needed extra gears.
